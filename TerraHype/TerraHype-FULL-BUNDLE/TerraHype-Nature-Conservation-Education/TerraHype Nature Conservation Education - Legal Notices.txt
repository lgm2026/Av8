1. ABOUT THIS APP
-----------------
TerraHype Nature Conservation Education ("TerraHype," "the app") is a free,
offline educational study tool about North American nature and conservation.
It is published and maintained by its operator under the alias "openNatureDB"
(referred to here as "the operator"). The app teaches introductory concepts in
ecology, botany, zoology, geology, habitats, conservation, and field methods
through bite-sized lessons, quizzes, illustrations, and reference material.

The app runs entirely on your device. It requires no account, collects no
personal information, and stores your progress only in your own web browser's
local storage on the device you use. The operator does not receive, transmit,
or have access to your name, your progress, or any usage data. Clearing your
browser data, or using the in-app reset, will permanently erase your saved
progress.

4. IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION
-------------------------------
Nature can be hazardous. Please read and follow these warnings:

  * NEVER eat, taste, or handle any wild mushroom, plant, berry, nut, root, or
    fungus based on information in this app. Many wild species are poisonous,
    and some deadly species closely resemble edible ones. Positive
    identification of anything you intend to eat requires a qualified local
    expert. This app must never be used to decide whether something wild is
    safe to consume.

  * Do not touch, capture, feed, corner, or disturb wild animals. Many can
    bite, sting, scratch, or carry disease, and disturbing wildlife can harm
    both you and the animal. Observe from a safe, respectful distance.

  * Be aware that some plants (such as poison ivy, poison oak, and others) and
    some animals are venomous, toxic, or otherwise dangerous to touch.

  * Respect all laws and regulations. Collecting rocks, minerals, fossils,
    plants, or animals, and entering certain lands, may be restricted or
    illegal. Many parks, preserves, and protected sites prohibit removing or
    disturbing natural and cultural materials. Always obtain permission and
    follow local, state, tribal, and federal rules.

  * Practice "Leave No Trace": stay on trails, pack out everything you bring
    in, and leave plants, rocks, fossils, and wildlife where you find them.

  * Always prioritize your own safety outdoors: tell someone your plans, watch
    the weather and terrain, carry water, and supervise children. Minors
    should explore nature only with a responsible adult.
